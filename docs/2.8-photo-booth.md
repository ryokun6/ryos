# Photo Booth

Photo Booth is a fun and interactive application for ryOS that allows you to capture memorable moments using your webcam. Apply a variety of creative visual effects to your photos before saving and sharing them with ease.

## Overview

The Photo Booth app transforms your ryOS desktop into a personal photo studio. It provides a simple yet powerful interface for taking still images and applying a range of dynamic visual effects in real-time. Whether you want to snap a quick selfie, create a multi-shot sequence, or experiment with quirky distortions and filters, Photo Booth makes the process intuitive and enjoyable.

Designed for quick captures and creative expression, Photo Booth integrates seamlessly with your system's webcam. It's perfect for casual use, adding a personal touch to your profile pictures, or simply having a bit of fun with friends and family. All photos are automatically saved and easily accessible for viewing and download directly within the app.

## Features

*   **Instant Photo Capture**: Quickly take single photos with a dedicated camera button.
*   **Timed Photo Sequences**: Capture a series of four photos automatically with a built-in timer, perfect for dynamic shots.
*   **Real-time Visual Effects**: Apply a diverse collection of visual effects, filters, and distortions to your live camera feed, enhancing your photos with creative flair.
*   **Photo Strip Management**: View all your captured photos in an organized strip at the bottom of the window, with options to show or hide it.
*   **Easy Photo Download**: Download any saved photo to your ryOS file system with a single click from the photo strip.
*   **Multiple Camera Support**: Seamlessly switch between available webcams connected to your system.
*   **Automatic Saving**: All photos taken are automatically saved within the application for later access.

## User Guide

### Getting Started
To launch Photo Booth, simply locate its icon in your ryOS Applications menu or dock and click it. Once open, the app will automatically attempt to access your default webcam and display its feed.

### Key Actions

*   **Taking a Single Photo**: To capture a single image, click the large red camera button located prominently in the application's interface.
*   **Capturing Quick Snaps**: For a sequence of photos, click the timer button, typically positioned to the left of the main camera button. This will initiate a countdown and capture four photos one second apart.
*   **Applying Visual Effects**: To explore and apply fun visual effects, look for an effects button (often represented by a palette or magic wand icon). Clicking this will reveal a selection of filters and distortions that you can apply in real-time to your webcam feed.
*   **Viewing Saved Photos**: Click the photo stack button, usually located on the far left, to toggle the visibility of the photo strip at the bottom of the window. This strip displays all the photos you've taken during the current session.
*   **Downloading a Photo**: Photos are automatically saved within the app. To download a specific photo to your ryOS file system, simply click on its thumbnail in the photo strip.
*   **Switching Cameras**: If you have multiple webcams connected, you can switch between them using the camera selection option, typically found in a menu or as a dedicated button.

### Tips & Shortcuts
*   Experiment with different effects before taking your photo to see how they look in real-time.
*   The timed photo sequence is great for capturing movement or group shots where you need a moment to pose.
*   Keep your photo strip visible to easily review your recent captures and quickly download your favorites.

## Technical Details

### Window Configuration
- Default size: 644×510px
- Minimum size: 644×510px
- Maximum size: 644×510px

### Component Architecture
The app consists of 2 component file(s):

- `src/apps/photo-booth/components/PhotoBoothComponent.tsx`
- `src/apps/photo-booth/components/PhotoBoothMenuBar.tsx`

### Hooks & Utilities
**Custom Hooks:**
- `src/apps/photo-booth/hooks/usePhotoBoothLogic.ts`

No custom utility files are used.

### State Management
Photo Booth primarily manages its internal application state using local component state and a custom hook, `usePhotoBoothLogic.ts`. This hook encapsulates the complex logic for camera stream management, photo capture, effect application, timer functionality, and the storage and retrieval of captured photos, ensuring a cohesive and responsive user experience.
